SHIMLA

Shimla, the capital city of Himachal Pradesh, is a popular hill station nestled in the northern Indian Himalayas. Known for its picturesque landscapes, colonial architecture, and pleasant climate, it attracts tourists year-round.


    Attractive place

  1. The Ridge: A scenic open space offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and a lively atmosphere with shops and cultural events.
  2. Mall Road: The main street of Shimla, perfect for leisurely walks, shopping, and enjoying local cuisine.
  3. Jakhoo Temple: A famous temple located atop Jakhoo Hill with a massive statue of Lord Hanuman and beautiful views of the city.
  4. Kufri: Known for adventure activities like skiing and tobogganing, with breathtaking views of the snow-capped mountains.
  5. Christ Church: A majestic neo-gothic church located on The Ridge, famous for its stained glass windows and serene atmosphere.

    Local foods

  • Chana Madra: A traditional Himachali dish made with chickpeas in a yogurt-based gravy and flavorful spices.
  • Siddu: Steamed wheat flour dough stuffed with ground nuts and spices, usually served with ghee.
  • Dham: A festive meal made with rice, lentils, and vegetables, often served at religious gatherings.
  • Tibetan Momos: Dumplings filled with either meat or vegetables, a popular street food in Shimla, served steamed or fried.
  • Babru: A Himachali stuffed paratha made with black gram flour, served with chutney or yogurt.
